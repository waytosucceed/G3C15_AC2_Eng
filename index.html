Subject and Predicate


Convention/Grammar

Subject: The noun that tells what or who the sentence is about. It is the main focus.

Predicate: The verb part that describes the action of the subject or gives details about it.

Compound Subject: When a sentence has two or more nouns joined together to act as the combined subject.

Compound Predicate: When a sentence contains two or more verbs or verb phrases joined together to act as the combined predicate or action.

Examples:

Maya and Ethan played soccer after school. Maya and Ethan played soccer after school
The bright sun melted the snow quickly. The bright sun pmelted the snow quickly

Sentence: The children scattered the rose petals along the aisle for the wedding ceremony.
